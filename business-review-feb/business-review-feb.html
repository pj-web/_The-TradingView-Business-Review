Thanks for checking in with our latest Business Review newsletter, where we cover major company and product updates involving our esteemed business partners.

New integrations and partnerships

 

Trading integration with Webull is live

 

In a new round of expanding trading opportunities for our users, we welcomed Webull, a major US broker with a worldwide presence and a shared spirit for innovation. 

 

With a Webull account, TradingView users can trade stocks, depository receipts, and ETFs and OTC markets. The integration includes 10,000 instruments from NASDAQ, NYSE, and NYSE Arca.

 

Visit the Webull broker profile on TradingView for more information about their offerings, or go directly to the Trading Panel on charts to connect to the integration.

 
 
Webull profile
 
 
 

All in for widgets in the new B3 client hub

 

Brazilian Stock Exchange B3 is Brazil's most significant infrastructure firm that fosters the country's growth by connecting and facilitating the financial and capital markets.

 

Already a long-time partner on technologies for the B3 media website, we extended our collaboration in a new direction with several widgets now empowering their new investors’ hub.

 

The hub, fast-growing in popularity, comprises analytical dashboards and summary reports on trading and markets, so the widgets perfectly complement the setup.

 
 
B3 Investor Area
 
 
 

Multi-tiered collaboration with Boursier.com

 

Boursier.com is a renowned French financial media outlet backed by the LVMH group.

 

As part of this partnership, they have integrated Lightweight Charts (LWC) and Advanced Charts (AC) into their website to provide visitors with a seamless charting and analysis experience.

 

Additionally, Boursier.com’s news feed is now incorporated into tradingview.com, solidifying our commitment to delivering best-on-the-market financial insights from trusted vendors.

 

Partner-specific personalisations accompanied all integrations, such as LWC getting individual chart styles and AC being modified with custom timeframes and tooltips.

 
 
Boursier.com
 
 
 

KB Securities goes for Trading Edition

 

KB Securities is one of the biggest Investment Banks in Korea, with $4 billion in capital and $100 billion in investor assets.

 

As a part of the partnership extension, kbsec.com upgraded the setup from Advanced Charts to Trading Edition, adding full-fledged trading capabilities and more charting features to its offering.

 

In this mutually strategic move, we aim to empower KB Securities' regional business goals and deliver premier charting and trading experience to a larger audience in Korean markets.

 
 
kbsec.com
